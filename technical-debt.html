Service · Technical Debt

Cut the cruft in your NetSuite account.

Years of customizations don't go away on their own. SuiteScript 1.0 still running. Bundles installed by partners who left. Workflows nobody dares touch. We inventory it, rate it, and retire what shouldn't be there.

What technical debt looks like in NetSuite

  • SuiteScript 1.0 user event scripts still running on critical records
  • Custom records duplicating native functionality (because someone didn't know better in 2017)
  • Bundles from former partners — undocumented, unpatched, unowned
  • Saved searches with hardcoded internal IDs that break every sandbox refresh
  • SuiteFlows that "you can't change because something might break"

Our remediation method

  • Inventory. Every script, workflow, custom record, bundle, and integration — cataloged with owner, last-touched date, and business value.
  • Score. Each item gets a complexity score and a value score. The matrix tells us what to retire, refactor, or leave alone.
  • Roadmap. Quarter-by-quarter plan. Quick wins this month, structural changes over six months.
  • Refactor & retire. Replace SuiteScript 1.0 with 2.1, consolidate workflows, document what stays.
Why now? Every quarter you wait, the cost of the next NetSuite upgrade, sandbox refresh, or audit grows. Technical debt compounds — like all debt.
